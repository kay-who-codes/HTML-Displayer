Case Primary Function(s) Examples
Nominative Subject of the sentence; Predicate nominative (used with verbs like "to be"). ὁ ἄνθρωπος καλός ἐστιν. (The man is good.)
ὁ ἄνθρωπος ("the man") is the subject, with the ending -ος.
ἡ γυνή ἐστι σοφή. (The woman is wise.)
ἡ γυνή ("the woman") ends in -ή, indicating the nominative case.
Genitive Expresses possession ("of"); Origin; Separation; Object of certain prepositions. τοῦ ἀνθρώπου ὁ φίλος ἔρχεται. (The friend of the man comes.)
τοῦ ἀνθρώπου ("of the man") has the genitive ending -ου.
ἡ κλῆσις τοῦ ἀνδρός. (The calling of the man.)
τοῦ ἀνδρός ("of the man") uses the genitive ending -ός.
Dative Indicates the indirect object ("to" or "for"); Instrumentality ("by" or "with"); Location; Object of certain prepositions. δίδωσι τῷ παιδίον βιβλίον. (He gives the child a book.)
τῷ παιδίον ("to the child") uses the dative -ῳ.
ἐν τῇ ἀγορᾷ. (In the marketplace.)
τῇ ἀγορᾷ ("in the marketplace") has the dative -ᾳ.
τῷ θεῷ δοῦλον πέμπει. (He sends a servant to the god.)
Accusative Direct object of the verb; Motion towards a place; Object of certain prepositions. βλέπει τὸν ἄνθρωπον. (He sees the man.)
τὸν ἄνθρωπον ("the man") ends in -ον, marking it as accusative.
εἰς τὴν πόλιν. (Into the city.)
τὴν πόλιν ("the city") uses -ιν to indicate motion towards.
τὴν οἰκίαν εἰσέρχεται. (He enters the house.)
Vocative Direct address (used when speaking to someone or something). ὦ φίλε, τί ποιεῖς; (O friend, what are you doing?)
φίλε ("friend") uses the vocative -ε.
ὦ Ζεῦ, σῶσον ἡμᾶς. (O Zeus, save us.)
ὦ Ζεῦ ("O Zeus") is in the vocative, with Ζεῦ marking direct address.
